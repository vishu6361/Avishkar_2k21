ElectromaniaFPGA Design event

Reinvent future with all programmable devices!

About

An event to design with programmable logic devices like FPGA. So gear up with your innovative idea and get ready to make your own chip. In this event the very basics of your digital electronics concepts and your verilog coding skills will be tested.

Participation Criteria

  • Team Size : 3
  • Year Participating:
    • B.tech 2nd year
    • B.tech 3rd year
    • M.Tech 1st year
  • Outside participation not Allowed
  • Interyear not allowed
  • ECE and EE only allowed

Contact Info

  • Saurabh Kumar Yadav
    • 8318623674
    • saurabh99184@gmail.com
  • Ankit Yadav
    • 7266857747
    • ankit0505yadav@gmail.com

Rules

1. The competition will consist of three rounds.

2. The first round, the preliminaries, will consist of the submission of an abstract that details the approach adopted by the team towards solving the problem and any progress made already. The abstract should clearly outline the following details:

  • Team details (members and their institutions with their branch, contact details including phone numbers and email addresses of each member and teamname).
  • Approach or algorithm to be used for suggesting a solution to the problem statement using flowchart or pseudo code
  • Work already completed (in which case a snapshot of current code isrequired).
  • Any other comments or Add-ons to be included.
  • Abstracts should be mail to any one of the co-ordinators listed below.

3. The second round will be on spot synthesis round. This round will test the Verilog HDL coding skills of the participating teams. Problem statements will be provided on spot and the teams are supposed to design and synthesize their solutions on spot. The points obtained in this round will be given appropriate weightage. Also there will be a quiz containing questions related to Digital Electronics, STA,VERILOG,FPGA,CMOS etc.

4. The final round of the competition will consist primarily of a presentation by the team, involving the following:

  • A test run on the reference software simulator
  • Design decisions and implementation details
  • A Viva round
